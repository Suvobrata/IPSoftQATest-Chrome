<div style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 150px;" class="thumbinner mp-thumb"><a title="Johnson Creek, about 100 yards from its confluence with the Willamette River in Milwaukie, Oregon" class="image" href="/wiki/File:JohnsonCreek1.jpg"><img width="150" height="100" data-file-height="1360" data-file-width="2040" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/JohnsonCreek1.jpg/225px-JohnsonCreek1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/JohnsonCreek1.jpg/300px-JohnsonCreek1.jpg 2x" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/JohnsonCreek1.jpg/150px-JohnsonCreek1.jpg" alt="Johnson Creek, about 100 yards from its confluence with the Willamette River in Milwaukie, Oregon" /></a></div>

<p><b><a title="Johnson Creek (Willamette River)" href="/wiki/Johnson_Creek_%28Willamette_River%29">Johnson Creek</a></b> is a 25-mile (40 km) tributary of the <a title="Willamette River" href="/wiki/Willamette_River">Willamette River</a> in the <a title="Portland metropolitan area" href="/wiki/Portland_metropolitan_area">Portland metropolitan area</a> of the U.S. state of <a title="Oregon" href="/wiki/Oregon">Oregon</a>. Part of the <a title="Drainage basin" href="/wiki/Drainage_basin">drainage basin</a> of the <a title="Columbia River" href="/wiki/Columbia_River">Columbia River</a>, its watershed covers 54 square miles (140 km<sup>2</sup>) of mostly urban land occupied by about 180,000 people. The creek flows generally west from the foothills of the <a title="Cascade Range" href="/wiki/Cascade_Range">Cascade Range</a> through sediments deposited by glacial floods on a substrate of <a title="Basalt" href="/wiki/Basalt">basalt</a>. Though <a title="Polluted" class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polluted">polluted</a>, it provides habitat for <a title="Salmon" href="/wiki/Salmon">salmon</a> and other <a title="Fish migration" href="/wiki/Fish_migration">migrating fish</a> along its free-flowing <a title="Main stem" href="/wiki/Main_stem">main stem</a>. Prior to European settlement, the heavily forested watershed was used by <a title="Native Americans in the United States" href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States">Native Americans</a> of the <a title="Chinookan peoples" href="/wiki/Chinookan_peoples">Chinook band</a> for fishing and hunting. In the 19th century, white <a title="Settler" href="/wiki/Settler">settlers</a> cleared much of the land for farming. The stream is named for William Johnson, a settler who in 1846 built a water-powered <a title="Sawmill" href="/wiki/Sawmill">sawmill</a> along the creek. By the early 20th century, a rail line parallel to the stream encouraged further residential and commercial development. Damage from seasonal flooding grew as <a title="Urban density" href="/wiki/Urban_density">urban density</a> increased in the floodplain. <div id="mp-otd-img" style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;">
<div style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 100px;" class="thumbinner mp-thumb"><a title="Charlie Chaplin as &quot;The Tramp&quot;" class="image" href="/wiki/File:Charlie_Chaplin_%28cropped%29.jpg"><img width="100" height="150" data-file-height="1344" data-file-width="896" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Charlie_Chaplin_%28cropped%29.jpg/150px-Charlie_Chaplin_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Charlie_Chaplin_%28cropped%29.jpg/200px-Charlie_Chaplin_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Charlie_Chaplin_%28cropped%29.jpg/100px-Charlie_Chaplin_%28cropped%29.jpg" alt="Charlie Chaplin as &quot;The Tramp&quot;" /></a>

<ul>
<li><a title="1497" href="/wiki/1497">1497</a> – Supporters of the <a title="Dominican Order" href="/wiki/Dominican_Order">Dominican</a> priest <a title="Girolamo Savonarola" href="/wiki/Girolamo_Savonarola">Girolamo Savonarola</a> collected and <b><a title="Bonfire of the Vanities" href="/wiki/Bonfire_of_the_Vanities">publicly burned</a></b> thousands of objects such as cosmetics, art, and books in <a title="Florence" href="/wiki/Florence">Florence</a>, Italy.</li>
<li><a title="1813" href="/wiki/1813">1813</a> – <a title="Napoleonic Wars" href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a>: Two evenly matched <a title="Frigates" class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frigates">frigates</a> from the <a title="French Navy" href="/wiki/French_Navy">French Navy</a> and the British <a title="Royal Navy" href="/wiki/Royal_Navy">Royal Navy</a> <b><a title="Action of 7 February 1813" href="/wiki/Action_of_7_February_1813">fought for four hours</a></b>, causing significant damage, but resulting in a stalemate.</li>
<li><a title="1914" href="/wiki/1914">1914</a> – The film <i><a title="Kid Auto Races at Venice" href="/wiki/Kid_Auto_Races_at_Venice">Kid Auto Races at Venice</a></i>, featuring the first appearance of comedy actor <b><a title="Charlie Chaplin" href="/wiki/Charlie_Chaplin">Charlie Chaplin</a></b><span style="padding-left:0.1em;" class="nowrap">'</span>s character "<a title="The Tramp" href="/wiki/The_Tramp">The Tramp</a>" <i>(pictured)</i>, was released.</li>
<li><a title="1943" href="/wiki/1943">1943</a> – <a title="World War II" href="/wiki/World_War_II">World War II</a>: Japan successfully <b><a title="Operation Ke" href="/wiki/Operation_Ke">withdrew its troops</a></b> from <a title="Guadalcanal" href="/wiki/Guadalcanal">Guadalcanal</a>.</li>
<li><a title="1948" href="/wiki/1948">1948</a> – <b><a title="Neil Harvey" href="/wiki/Neil_Harvey">Neil Harvey</a></b> became the youngest <a title="Cricket in Australia" href="/wiki/Cricket_in_Australia">Australian</a> to score a <a title="Century (cricket)" href="/wiki/Century_%28cricket%29">century</a> in <a title="Test cricket" href="/wiki/Test_cricket">Test cricket</a>.</li>
</ul>
